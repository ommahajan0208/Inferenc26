Issues found in current code

Bug 2: Ignoring timing data critical

The CSV has finishing times for all 8 horses per race, not just the winner. The code only reads the winner column, discarding 87.5% of available information.

Bug 3: Wrong optimization objective critical

Kelly maximizes compound log-wealth growth. But the score = average profit per race (fixed bets, not compounding). These require completely different strategies.

Win probabilities: frequency counting vs monte carlo from fitted distributions

Fitting Normal distributions to all 8 horses' lap times and running 10M Monte Carlo races gives more reliable estimates than counting wins in only 500 races. Correlations between horses are near zero, confirming independence.

Expected value with correct market fractions

The market severely underestimates Shadowfax (true 32.5% vs 8% implied) and Morningstar (32% vs 11%). These are the massive edges to exploit.

Strategy showdown: expected profit per race (simulated 100k races)

Key insight: E[profit/race] = Σ bᵢ × EVᵢ. This is linear in bets. The optimal solution is to put the entire £10,000 on the single horse with the highest EV — Shadowfax at +246%. Kelly is the right tool for compounding wealth, not for maximizing a fixed-bet average score.
